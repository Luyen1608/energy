Research on Assessing The Current Status And Proposing Solutions to Promote Energy Efficiency in the Food Processing Industry
The study conducted a survey through questionnaires with 96 enterprises in the food processing industry (mainly small and medium enterprises, with 27.1% being key energy users) focusing on the seafood and aquatic
This study evaluates energy use and consumption practices in the Food Processing Industry, focusing on available energy efficiency verification tools. It identifies barriers, challenges, and difficulties that hinder the adoption of energy-saving measures, while also investigating best practices and innovative solutions within the sector. The research includes mapping and analyzing key stakeholders involved in promoting energy-efficient practices and assessing existing support mechanisms and incentives available to enterprises. Based on these findings, the study proposes tailored support solutions to address the specific needs of enterprises, fostering the adoption of energy-saving measures and enhancing sustainability in the Food Processing Industry.
